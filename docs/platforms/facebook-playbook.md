# Facebook Editorial Playbook

**Version:** 1.0  
**Status:** Platform Editorial Standard  
**Scope:** PiercingConnect content adapted for Facebook (Page posts, links, optional video)

This playbook defines how verified PiercingConnect knowledge is transformed into Facebook-native content while preserving editorial integrity. It is subordinate to the PiercingConnect Editorial Style Guide.

---

## 1. Platform Purpose

Facebook is a community-oriented discovery and sharing channel. PiercingConnect uses it to deliver readable educational summaries that travel well in feeds and groups—helping friends and family share trustworthy aftercare context without oversimplifying safety.

---

## 2. Audience Intent

Users want a clear, self-contained explanation they can read in one sitting. They may share posts with someone who just got pierced. They value calm tone over hype and are wary of obvious ads disguised as advice.

---

## 3. User Behavior

- Reads post text before clicking links; long walls of text are skipped.
- Shares posts that feel helpful and non-judgmental.
- Comments ask practical questions; Page replies shape brand trust.
- Link posts depend on preview metadata; the post copy must stand alone if the preview fails.
- Older demographics may prefer full sentences over fragment-style social copy.

---

## 4. Content Goals

- Convert one verified source into a 150–300 word educational post (or structured link share) with explicit limits.
- Summarize formula, guidance alignment, advantages, and limitations—not only product praise.
- Preserve evidence boundaries in plain language.
- Encourage informed reading of the full PiercingConnect review, not immediate purchase.

---

## 5. Tone of Voice

Warm but professional. Complete sentences. No outrage, shame, or meme density. Write as a knowledgeable editor explaining to a curious adult—not a brand shouting into the feed.

---

## 6. Opening / Hook Rules

- First sentence answers “why should I care?” with a useful fact or distinction.
- Do not bury the educational point below a product name.
- Avoid engagement bait (“Share if you agree,” “Tag someone who…”).
- Hooks must not imply crisis unless the verified source addresses an actual safety alert.

---

## 7. Content Structure

**Standard educational post:**
1. Opening hook (distinction or reader situation).
2. What the product/topic is (one paragraph).
3. Formula or core fact + professional guidance context.
4. Practical advantages (brief, non-promotional).
5. Limitations and individual variation.
6. When to seek professional guidance.
7. Calm CTA to full review + affiliate disclosure if links present.

**Link share:** Post copy must summarize value even if the reader never clicks. Preview title/description should match editorial standards (no clickbait).

Optional bullet lists for scannability; avoid more than five bullets.

---

## 8. CTA Strategy

- Primary: “Read the full PiercingConnect review for evidence, alternatives, and limits.”
- Secondary: “Share with someone researching aftercare” only when the post is purely educational.
- Never: urgency, discount language, or outcome guarantees.
- Link CTAs must go to canonical PiercingConnect URLs, not intermediary affiliate wrappers without disclosure.

---

## 9. Evergreen Strategy

Facebook posts should remain accurate without “this week” framing unless covering verified news.

- Avoid event-tied hooks (holidays, back-to-school) unless tied to timeless aftercare teaching.
- Pin evergreen explainer posts that link to cornerstone reviews.
- When updating source articles, add a comment or edit note on high-traffic Facebook posts rather than leaving stale claims.
- Repost evergreen content only after editorial re-verification, not on autopilot.

---

## 10. News / Trend Strategy

Use Facebook for verified updates: formulation changes, safety notices, or guidance revisions reflected in the source record.

- Lead with what changed; link to updated PiercingConnect article.
- After the news cycle, publish or boost an evergreen summary post that incorporates the durable lesson.
- Do not comment on unverified viral stories or celebrity piercing drama.
- Group sharing: ensure posts remain factual when context is stripped in reshares.

---

## 11. Affiliate Integration Rules

- Include disclosure in post body when linking to commission-bearing pages: plain language, visible before the link.
- Educational content precedes the link.
- Do not use Facebook’s promotional post tools to bypass disclosure requirements.
- Independence statement required: review conclusions are not determined by commission.

---

## 12. Things to Avoid

- Copy-pasting entire articles into the post.
- All-caps warnings or infection fear imagery.
- Diagnostic comments in replies (“that’s definitely infected”).
- Political or unrelated trending topics to boost reach.
- Withholding limitations to improve engagement.
- Fake scarcity or “limited time” affiliate framing.

---

## 13. Success Metrics

- Link clicks to canonical PiercingConnect articles (quality traffic).
- Shares and saves relative to reach (utility signal).
- Comment sentiment and question resolution rate.
- Hide/report rate (should stay low; high rate signals tone or accuracy problems).
- Post corrections required after publish (minimize).
- Return visits from Facebook referrals to multiple articles (trust depth).

---

## 14. Approval Checklist

- [ ] Post derived from one verified source; word count appropriate for Facebook.
- [ ] Opening hook is educational, not sensational.
- [ ] Formula, guidance, advantages, and limitations all represented fairly.
- [ ] Evidence boundaries preserved; no new medical advice.
- [ ] Escalation guidance included for health-adjacent topics.
- [ ] No guaranteed healing or universal suitability.
- [ ] Affiliate disclosure present if applicable.
- [ ] CTA directs to full review, not purchase-first language.
- [ ] Evergreen framing confirmed; news posts flagged for follow-up evergreen asset.
- [ ] Human editorial approval before publish.
