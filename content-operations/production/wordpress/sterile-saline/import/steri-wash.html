Editorial Summary

Steri-Wash Sterile Saline Piercing Spray / Wash is a sterile saline piercing spray-mist sold on the official Steri-Wash storefront in 3 oz, 4.25 oz, and 8 oz sizes. Official features list no drugs or preservatives, 360° dispensing, sterility maintained throughout canister life, and spray ingredients as Water & Sodium Chloride.

This review evaluates Steri-Wash against declared criteria—ingredient transparency, SKU-specific concentration evidence, sterility/preservative claims, format, and overlap or gaps versus Association of Professional Piercers (APP) aftercare guidance. It is not a ranked “best saline” verdict and does not guarantee healing outcomes.

Important evidence boundary (SKU-scoped): Official 4.25 oz product back label evidence lists Purified Water and 0.9% USP NaCl and describes an isotonic solution. That 0.9% claim is not verified for 3 oz or 8 oz sizes on materials consulted—do not treat it as a full-line fact. For category literacy, start with the Sterile Saline Complete Guide.

Evidence and Guideline Alignment

Professional guidance (category layer) — app-aftercare

APP Suggested Aftercare for Body Piercings strongly encourages sterile saline labeled for wound wash, with ingredients listing 0.9% sodium chloride as the only ingredient (sometimes purified water). APP advises against DIY sea-salt mixes, harsh cleaners, over-cleaning, and using contact-lens saline, nasal spray, or eye drops. APP does not endorse any specific brand.

FAQ

Q: Is Steri-Wash 0.9% sodium chloride?
A: Yes for the verified 4.25 oz back label (Purified Water, 0.9% USP NaCl; isotonic label wording). Not verified here for 3 oz or 8 oz. Check the can you buy. Do not rely on wholesale “.09%” marketing copy as proof.

Q: How often should I use it?
A: APP guidance does not give a fixed universal daily schedule for all piercings. Follow your piercer and the directions on the specific Steri-Wash SKU.

Q: Is it the same as APP-preferred packaged sterile saline?
A: It overlaps on packaged sterile saline, water + sodium chloride, no drugs/preservatives, and spray use. 0.9% is documented on the 4.25 oz label; full-line concentration match is unresolved. APP does not endorse brands.

Q: Can I use homemade salt water instead?
A: APP suggested aftercare no longer recommends DIY sea-salt mixes; they are commonly too salty and may over-dry the piercing. Prefer packaged sterile saline wound wash where available.

Q: What if I have irritation or worry about infection?
A: Do not self-diagnose from an article. Contact a piercer or healthcare provider for concerning or worsening symptoms.
