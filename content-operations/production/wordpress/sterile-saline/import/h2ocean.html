Editorial Summary

H2Ocean Piercing Aftercare Spray is a sea-salt and lysozyme aftercare spray. Official materials list four ingredients: Deionized/Reverse Osmosis Purified Water, Sea Salt, Lysozyme, and Sodium Citrate. Marketing positions Red Sea salt with natural lysozyme and frames the product as a saline solution spray for piercings—sea-salt-based, not simple isotonic sodium chloride. Official shop offers 1.5 oz and 4 oz variants.

This review evaluates H2Ocean against declared criteria—ingredient transparency, how manufacturer claims are labeled, size availability, and explicit comparison to Association of Professional Piercers (APP) packaged sterile 0.9% sodium chloride wound-wash guidance. It is not a ranked “best saline” verdict and does not guarantee healing outcomes.

Evidence and Guideline Alignment

Professional guidance (category layer) — app-aftercare

APP Suggested Aftercare for Body Piercings strongly encourages sterile saline labeled for wound wash, with ingredients listing 0.9% sodium chloride as the only ingredient (sometimes purified water). APP advises against DIY sea-salt mixes, harsh cleaners, over-cleaning, and using contact-lens saline, nasal spray, or eye drops. APP does not endorse any specific brand.

FAQ

Q: Is H2Ocean the same as sterile 0.9% sodium chloride wound wash?
A: Not verified. Official formula is purified water, sea salt, lysozyme, and sodium citrate. Sterile 0.9% NaCl isotonic saline labeling is unsupported in the evidence layer.

Q: Does APP recommend or endorse H2Ocean?
A: APP does not endorse brands. APP encourages sterile saline labeled for wound wash with 0.9% sodium chloride (sometimes with purified water). H2Ocean’s documented formula differs from that baseline.

Q: How often should I use it?
A: Official shop meta copy recommends 3–6 times daily to remove discharge and debris—that is manufacturer marketing, not APP body-piercing schedule guidance. APP does not specify a fixed universal daily count and warns against over-cleaning. Follow your piercer and the product label; do not treat this review as dosing instructions.

Q: Can I use homemade salt water instead?
A: APP suggested aftercare no longer recommends DIY sea-salt mixes; they are commonly too salty and may over-dry the piercing. Prefer packaged sterile saline wound wash where that is the piercer-recommended approach.

Q: What if I have irritation or worry about infection?
A: Do not self-diagnose from an article. Contact a piercer or healthcare provider for concerning or worsening symptoms.
