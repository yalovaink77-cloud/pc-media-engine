# PiercingConnect Editorial Style Guide

**Version:** 1.0  
**Status:** Official Editorial Standard  
**Scope:** All published content on PiercingConnect

This document is the authoritative editorial constitution for PiercingConnect. It defines how every article must be written, reviewed, and approved. It applies equally to human editors, automated drafting systems, and future contributors. It is not a prompt, a technical specification, or a workflow manual.

---

## 1. Mission

PiercingConnect exists to help people make informed, safe decisions about body piercing—before, during, and after the procedure.

Our mission is to publish accurate, practical, and trustworthy guidance on piercing aftercare, healing, product selection, and common complications. We serve readers who may be anxious, inexperienced, or recovering from a problem. Our obligation is clarity, safety, and honesty—not traffic, conversion, or promotion.

Every article must leave the reader better equipped to protect their health and communicate effectively with qualified professionals when needed.

---

## 2. Editorial Philosophy

**Reader safety precedes publication speed.** No deadline justifies incomplete sourcing, vague medical advice, or unverified claims.

**Accuracy over amplification.** We do not sensationalize complications, exaggerate product benefits, or imply certainty where evidence is limited.

**Independence over revenue.** Commercial relationships must never determine conclusions, rankings, or recommendations.

**Specificity over generality.** Piercing care varies by location, jewelry, skin type, and healing stage. Articles must acknowledge context and avoid one-size-fits-all prescriptions.

**Revision is editorial duty.** When standards evolve or evidence changes, content must be updated, corrected, or retired—not left to decay.

**Transparency is non-negotiable.** Readers must always know who wrote or reviewed content, what evidence supports it, and when commercial interests are present.

---

## 3. Brand Voice

PiercingConnect speaks with **calm authority**: knowledgeable, direct, and respectful. We are neither clinical nor casual. We do not use fear to motivate action, nor hype to sell products.

| Attribute | We are | We are not |
|-----------|--------|------------|
| Tone | Reassuring, precise, measured | Alarmist, preachy, or dismissive |
| Register | Plain language with correct terminology | Slang-heavy or unnecessarily academic |
| Stance | Evidence-informed and practical | Speculative, anecdotal, or promotional |
| Audience | Adults seeking reliable guidance | Influencer-style or trend-chasing |

**Language conventions:**

- Use second person ("you") when giving practical instructions.
- Define technical terms on first use (e.g., *granuloma*, *migration*, *LITHA*).
- Prefer active voice and short sentences for instructions.
- Avoid absolutes ("always," "never," "guaranteed") unless supported by consensus guidance.
- Do not address the reader as a patient under our care; we provide information, not treatment.

---

## 4. Reader Promise

Every PiercingConnect article implicitly makes these promises:

1. **Safety first.** Content will not encourage harmful practices, DIY medical intervention, or delay of professional care when warranted.
2. **Honest limits.** We will state what we know, what we do not know, and when individual circumstances require professional judgment.
3. **Clear sourcing.** Factual claims will be traceable to identifiable, credible sources.
4. **Commercial honesty.** Affiliate links, sponsorships, and product relationships will be disclosed plainly.
5. **Accessible structure.** Key information will be findable without reading the entire article.
6. **Ongoing accuracy.** Material errors will be corrected promptly and noted when significant.

Breaking any of these promises is grounds for withholding publication or withdrawing content.

## 5. E-E-A-T Principles

PiercingConnect adheres to Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness as editorial requirements—not SEO tactics.

### Experience
Content about aftercare routines, product use, and healing timelines must reflect real-world piercing practice. Where first-hand experience informs the article, it must be labeled and subordinate to evidence and professional standards.

### Expertise
Medical, anatomical, and wound-healing claims require review by a qualified reviewer or citation of authoritative professional sources (e.g., APP guidelines, peer-reviewed literature, licensed practitioners).

### Authoritativeness
Articles must cite recognized authorities in piercing and dermatology. Anonymous or unverifiable sources are unacceptable for health-related claims.

### Trustworthiness
Bylines, review credits, correction policies, affiliate disclosures, and conflict-of-interest statements must be visible and accurate. Trust is built through consistency, not assertion.

**Minimum E-E-A-T requirements by content type:**

| Content type | Author requirement | Review requirement |
|--------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Aftercare guides | Experienced editor | Professional or senior editorial review |
| Safety / complication articles | Health-literate writer | Qualified medical or piercing professional review |
| Product reviews | Independent reviewer | Editorial fact-check |
| Comparison articles | Independent reviewer | Editorial + commercial independence check |

## 6. Medical & Safety Writing Standards

Piercing content intersects with wound care and dermatology. The following rules apply to all health-adjacent material.

### Scope of advice
- Publish **information and general guidance**, not individualized medical diagnosis or treatment plans.
- Never instruct readers to ignore infection signs, severe pain, allergic reactions, or structural jewelry problems.
- Always include clear criteria for when to seek professional care (piercer, physician, or emergency services as appropriate).

### Prohibited content
- Instructions for self-surgery, unsanctioned jewelry removal from embedded piercings, or dangerous home remedies (e.g., harsh chemicals, excessive manipulation).
- Definitive diagnostic language ("you have a keloid") without qualifying that only a professional can diagnose.
- Recommendations to avoid medical care when symptoms warrant it.

### Required elements for safety content
- **Symptom description** — what the reader may observe.
- **Likely causes** — stated with appropriate uncertainty.
- **Safe self-care boundaries** — what may be reasonable at home vs. what is not.
- **Escalation triggers** — specific signs requiring professional attention.
- **Disclaimer** — concise statement that content is informational, not a substitute for professional medical advice.

### Terminology precision
- Distinguish *irritation*, *infection*, *granuloma*, *keloid*, and *migration*; do not use them interchangeably.
- Do not conflate "hypoallergenic" with "safe for all healing piercings."
- Use "contact your piercer or healthcare provider" rather than implying either alone is always sufficient.

## 7. Evidence Standards

Every factual claim must meet one of the following evidence tiers. Claims may not exceed the strength of their supporting tier.

### Tier 1 — Primary / Authoritative
Professional association guidelines, government health agencies, peer-reviewed studies, manufacturer instructions where relevant to product use.

### Tier 2 — Expert Consensus
Widely accepted professional practice documented in reputable trade or clinical publications, statements from credentialed practitioners with clear attribution.

### Tier 3 — Editorial Synthesis
Reasoned summary of Tier 1 and Tier 2 sources. Must not introduce new factual claims.

### Tier 4 — Excluded from factual claims
Forums, social media anecdotes, unverified personal testimony, AI-generated citations, and unattributed "studies show" statements.

**Citation rules:**
- Attribute specific claims to named sources.
- Prefer primary sources over aggregators.
- Do not cite sources that were not consulted.
- When evidence is mixed or emerging, say so explicitly.
- Statistics require a named source and date context.

**Placeholder and unresolved references are publication blockers.**

## 8. Commercial Transparency & Affiliate Ethics

PiercingConnect may earn revenue through affiliate links, advertising, and sponsored placements. Revenue must never compromise editorial integrity.

### Disclosure
- Every article containing affiliate links must include a **clear, conspicuous disclosure** near the top of the article and adjacent to commercial recommendations where practical.
- Disclosures must use plain language (e.g., "We may earn a commission if you purchase through links on this page").
- Sponsored content must be labeled as such before the reader encounters promotional material.

### Independence
- Affiliate relationships must not determine product inclusion, ranking, or verdict.
- Editors must not accept compensation for positive coverage without explicit sponsorship labeling.
- Products recommended must meet the same review standards regardless of commission availability.

### Recommendation integrity
- Recommend only products the editorial team would stand behind independent of commission.
- If a product has known limitations, state them even when it is an affiliate product.
- Do not use urgency tactics ("buy now before it's too late") or false scarcity.

### Suitability language
- Product recommendations must align with stated limitations (e.g., "suitable for healed piercings only" must not appear alongside language implying use on fresh wounds unless evidence supports it).
- Contradictory suitability statements are publication blockers.

## 9. Product Review Standard

Product reviews help readers choose aftercare and jewelry products. Each review must be **independent, structured, and reproducible in reasoning**.

### Required structure
1. **Product identification** — name, manufacturer, intended use.
2. **Who it is for** — healing stage, piercing type, sensitivities.
3. **Who it is not for** — explicit exclusions.
4. **Key attributes** — ingredients, material, format, size, price range (approximate if necessary).
5. **Performance assessment** — based on stated criteria, not hype.
6. **Limitations and drawbacks** — mandatory section; no product is flawless.
7. **Verdict** — concise summary aligned with evidence and experience.
8. **Affiliate disclosure** — if applicable.

### Assessment criteria
Reviews must evaluate products against declared criteria (e.g., ingredient safety, ease of use, value, suitability for sensitive skin). Criteria must be stated before conclusions.

### Prohibited practices
- Unverified performance claims ("best on the market") without defined comparative basis.
- Reviewing products not examined or thoroughly researched.
- Hiding material defects or incompatibility.
- Copying manufacturer marketing language verbatim.

### Updates
Reviews must be revisited when formulations change, safety advisories are issued, or products are discontinued. Outdated reviews must be updated or archived.

## 10. Comparative Review Standard

Comparison articles (e.g., "Saline Spray A vs. B") require heightened rigor because readers use them to make decisions.

### Methodology
- State comparison criteria before presenting results.
- Compare like with like (same product category, similar intended use).
- Apply identical criteria to each product.
- Disclose affiliate relationships affecting any product in the comparison.

### Ranking and verdicts
- Rankings must follow declared criteria; arbitrary ordering is prohibited.
- If no clear winner exists, say so. Ties and qualified recommendations are acceptable.
- "Best overall" labels require justification against every other product evaluated.

### Balance
- Each product must receive fair coverage of strengths and weaknesses.
- Do not omit eligible competitors to favor affiliate partners.
- Include a summary table when comparing three or more products.

### Limitations
- State scope limits (e.g., "We compared saline sprays only; soaks and oils are covered separately").
- Do not extrapolate comparison results beyond tested attributes.

## 11. SEO Editorial Rules

Search visibility serves discovery; it must not distort editorial quality.

### Principles
- Write for readers first. Headings, summaries, and structure should aid comprehension, not keyword insertion.
- Titles must accurately describe content. Clickbait is prohibited.
- Meta descriptions must summarize the article honestly.

### Permitted optimization
- Clear, descriptive headings that reflect user intent.
- Logical internal linking to related PiercingConnect articles.
- FAQ sections that answer genuine reader questions with substantive answers—not thin content for indexing.

### Prohibited optimization
- Keyword stuffing or unnatural repetition.
- Hidden text, doorway content, or pages that exist only to capture queries without serving readers.
- Publishing substantially duplicate content across URLs.
- Bait-and-switch titles that do not match article body.

### Technical editorial responsibility
Editors must ensure canonical intent: one authoritative URL per topic where possible. Major updates should be reflected in the live article, not fragmented across near-duplicate pages.

## 12. AI Usage Policy

Automated systems may assist research, drafting, and formatting. They do not replace editorial judgment, professional review, or accountability.

### Permitted uses
- Research summarization under human verification.
- Structural drafting subject to full human rewrite and fact-check.
- Grammar, clarity, and consistency editing.
- Metadata generation subject to human approval.

### Prohibited uses
- Publishing AI-generated content without human review and approval.
- Relying on automated systems for medical claims, product verdicts, or safety guidance without qualified human verification.
- Using automated tools to generate fictitious citations, testimonials, or experience claims.
- Permitting automated systems to override this style guide.

### Verification requirement
All AI-assisted content must be treated as unverified until a human editor confirms accuracy, sourcing, compliance with this guide, and appropriateness for publication.

### Attribution
PiercingConnect does not present AI systems as authors or reviewers. Bylines and review credits reflect human accountability.

### System neutrality
This policy applies regardless of which tools contributors use. No tool-specific instructions belong in this document.

## 13. Human Review Policy

No PiercingConnect article may be published without human editorial approval.

### Review stages

| Stage | Responsibility |
|-------|----------------|
| **Structural review** | Confirms outline, scope, and reader promise. |
| **Editorial review** | Confirms voice, clarity, completeness, and compliance with this guide. |
| **Fact-check** | Verifies claims, sources, product details, and links. |
| **Safety review** | Required for health-adjacent content; confirms escalation guidance and prohibited content absence. |
| **Commercial review** | Confirms disclosures, affiliate integrity, and suitability alignment. |
| **Final approval** | Authorized editor releases for publication. |

### Review outcomes
- **Approved** — ready for publication.
- **Revise** — specific corrections required; re-review mandatory.
- **Reject** — does not meet standards; may not be published in current form.

### Accountability
Reviewers must be named in internal records. Safety-critical articles must document qualified review. Reviewers are accountable for their approval.

### Emergency corrections
Published content found to contain safety errors, material factual errors, or compliance failures must be corrected immediately. Significant corrections must be noted publicly when appropriate.

## 14. Publishing Checklist

Every article must pass this checklist before publication. A single failure blocks release until resolved.

### Content integrity
- [ ] Title accurately reflects content.
- [ ] Article fulfills the reader promise for its content type.
- [ ] All factual claims meet evidence standards (Section 7).
- [ ] No placeholder, broken, or unresolved source references.
- [ ] Medical and safety content includes escalation guidance and disclaimer where required.
- [ ] Terminology is precise; complication terms are not conflated.

### Structure and quality
- [ ] Headings create a logical reading path.
- [ ] Key information is accessible without reading the full article.
- [ ] Instructions are actionable and unambiguous.
- [ ] Limitations and exclusions are stated where applicable.

### Commercial compliance
- [ ] Affiliate and sponsorship disclosures present and conspicuous.
- [ ] Product recommendations align with stated suitability.
- [ ] No contradictory commercial or safety language.
- [ ] Reviews and comparisons follow Sections 9 and 10.

### E-E-A-T and governance
- [ ] Author and reviewer credits are accurate.
- [ ] Required professional review completed for health-adjacent content.
- [ ] AI-assisted material fully verified by a human editor.
- [ ] Human final approval recorded.

### Publication standards
- [ ] Internal links functional and relevant.
- [ ] External citations link to intended authoritative sources.
- [ ] SEO elements (title, description, headings) are accurate, not deceptive.
- [ ] Content published as draft pending review unless explicitly approved for live release.

## 15. Gold Standard Article Definition

A **Gold Standard** PiercingConnect article is the benchmark against which all content is measured. It is publishable, trustworthy, durable, and reader-complete.

### Defining characteristics

**1. Purpose clarity**  
The reader knows within the first two paragraphs what the article covers, who it is for, and what they will learn.

**2. Safety completeness**  
Health-adjacent articles include symptom context, safe self-care boundaries, escalation triggers, and a clear statement that content is informational—not medical advice.

**3. Evidence discipline**  
Every factual claim is sourced or clearly labeled as general professional consensus. No tier-4 evidence presented as fact.

**4. Commercial honesty**  
Disclosures are plain and visible. Product verdicts are independent, with limitations acknowledged.

**5. Structural excellence**  
Logical headings, scannable summaries, proportional depth, and no filler. FAQ sections, if present, answer real questions substantively.

**6. Voice consistency**  
Calm, authoritative, precise. No fear-mongering, hype, or condescension.

**7. Decision utility**  
The reader can act on the article—or know exactly when to seek professional help instead.

**8. Review integrity**  
Human review is documented. Safety content has qualified review. AI assistance, if any, is fully verified.

**9. Factual durability**  
Written to remain accurate; product details, guidelines, and links are current at time of publication.

**10. Corrections readiness**  
Published with the expectation that it will be updated; ownership for maintenance is assigned.
