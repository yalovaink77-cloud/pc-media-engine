# Instagram Editorial Playbook

**Version:** 1.0  
**Status:** Platform Editorial Standard  
**Scope:** PiercingConnect content adapted for Instagram (Feed, Carousel, Reels, Stories)

This playbook defines how verified PiercingConnect knowledge is transformed into Instagram-native content while preserving editorial integrity. It is subordinate to the PiercingConnect Editorial Style Guide.

---

## 1. Platform Purpose

Instagram is a visual discovery and education channel. PiercingConnect uses it to help people understand piercing aftercare, safety, and product context in scannable, mobile-first formats—not to drive impulse purchases.

---

## 2. Audience Intent

Users arrive to learn quickly, compare options visually, and feel reassured. They may be new to piercing, researching aftercare, or checking whether a product fits professional guidance. They are not seeking a full clinical consultation in-app.

---

## 3. User Behavior

- Scrolls rapidly; stops for clear visuals and the first line of text.
- Saves carousels for later reference.
- Watches Reels with sound optional; reads on-screen text.
- Rarely reads long captions end-to-end unless hooked in the first sentence.
- Trusts creators who appear calm, specific, and honest about limits.

---

## 4. Content Goals

- Translate one verified source into a platform-native asset (carousel, Reel, Story, or static post).
- Teach one clear idea per post—not the entire article.
- Preserve evidence boundaries and professional guidance distinctions.
- Direct interested readers to the full PiercingConnect review or guide when depth is required.
- Build long-term trust through consistency, not viral spikes.

---

## 5. Tone of Voice

Calm authority. Plain language. Short sentences. No influencer hype, fear hooks, or trend-chasing slang. Speak as an informed editor, not a salesperson or clinician treating the viewer.

---

## 6. Opening / Hook Rules

- Lead with a useful distinction, question the audience actually has, or a common misconception—never alarm.
- First slide or first Reel frame must stand alone without the caption.
- Do not open with product name alone; open with the reader’s situation or learning need.
- Avoid “You NEED this,” “Stop doing X or else,” or engagement bait (“Comment YES if…”).
- Hooks must remain accurate; curiosity gaps must not misrepresent the source.

---

## 7. Content Structure

**Carousels (preferred for education):**
1. Hook — problem or distinction.
2. Context — what the topic covers.
3–5. Core teaching — formula, guidance, or practical boundary (one idea per slide).
6. Limits — uncertainty, individual variation, or when to seek help.
7. CTA — full review or guide on PiercingConnect.

**Reels:** Hook (0–3s) → explain (3–25s) → limits (25–40s) → CTA (final 5s). On-screen text required for key claims.

**Stories:** Single idea; link sticker to full article when available.

**Captions:** Expand the hook; include affiliate disclosure when links are present; state “not medical advice” for health-adjacent topics.

---

## 8. CTA Strategy

- Primary CTA: read the full PiercingConnect review or guide for formula, evidence, and limitations.
- Secondary CTA: save the post for aftercare reference.
- Never use urgency (“buy now,” “limited stock”) or imply purchase equals healing.
- CTAs must match the asset; do not promise outcomes the source does not support.

---

## 9. Evergreen Strategy

Evergreen Instagram assets teach durable concepts: sterile saline vs. DIY mixes, APP-aligned aftercare principles, when to seek professional guidance, product category fit—not fleeting trends.

- Design carousels and Reels to remain accurate without date references.
- Avoid seasonal hooks unless tied to verified, timeless guidance.
- Link evergreen posts to canonical PiercingConnect URLs; update captions when source articles change.
- Repost or refresh visuals only after editorial re-review, not on a fixed schedule alone.

---

## 10. News / Trend Strategy

Use news or trends only when they affect reader safety, product formulation changes, or authoritative guidance updates verified in the source record.

- News posts must cite what changed and what did not.
- Trending audio or formats are optional; accuracy is mandatory.
- When a news item has lasting value, fold its teaching into an evergreen carousel or Reel and archive the time-sensitive post.
- Do not chase piercing fads, celebrity piercings, or unverified “hack” content.

---

## 11. Affiliate Integration Rules

- Disclose plainly in caption when a post links to a page with affiliate links: e.g., “We may earn a commission from qualifying purchases; this review remains editorially independent.”
- Do not place affiliate CTAs before educational content.
- Product mentions must include limitations in the same post or carousel.
- Never let commission availability determine whether a product appears.

---

## 12. Things to Avoid

- Sensational before/after healing claims.
- Diagnostic language (“this is infected”).
- Guaranteed outcomes or healing timelines.
- Keyword-stuffed captions or hidden hashtags for medical terms.
- Copy-pasting article paragraphs into captions.
- Fear-based hooks or shame language.
- Engagement bait, polls that trivialize safety, or misleading “link in bio” promises.

---

## 13. Success Metrics

Measure trust and utility, not vanity alone:

- Saves and shares (reference intent).
- Profile visits and link clicks to full reviews.
- Completion rate on Reels (did viewers reach the limits/CTA section?).
- Comment quality (questions answered with source-aligned replies).
- Correction rate (how often posts require factual updates—lower is better).
- Downstream time on PiercingConnect articles from Instagram referrals.

---

## 14. Approval Checklist

- [ ] Derived from one verified PiercingConnect source; no new evidence added.
- [ ] Platform-native format (not a truncated article dump).
- [ ] Hook is accurate and non-sensational.
- [ ] Evidence boundaries preserved (manufacturer vs. professional guidance vs. what evidence supports).
- [ ] Limitations and escalation guidance included for health-adjacent content.
- [ ] No guaranteed healing or universal suitability claims.
- [ ] Affiliate disclosure present if links include commission.
- [ ] CTA points to full review/guide; not purchase-first.
- [ ] Evergreen assets contain no stale dates or trend-only framing.
- [ ] Human editorial approval recorded before publish.
