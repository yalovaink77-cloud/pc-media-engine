# X Editorial Playbook

**Version:** 1.0  
**Status:** Platform Editorial Standard  
**Scope:** PiercingConnect content adapted for X (posts, threads, quote posts)

This playbook defines how verified PiercingConnect knowledge is transformed into X-native content while preserving editorial integrity. It is subordinate to the PiercingConnect Editorial Style Guide.

---

## 1. Platform Purpose

X is a concise public-information channel. PiercingConnect uses it to break verified knowledge into precise, sequential thoughts—correcting misconceptions, stating evidence boundaries, and pointing to full reviews when depth is required.

---

## 2. Audience Intent

Users scan quickly for clarity and credibility. They may follow for piercing education, share threads with friends, or discover PiercingConnect through search. They distrust threads that read like ad copy or outrage bait.

---

## 3. User Behavior

- Reads the first post only unless the hook earns the thread.
- Expects one idea per post; dense paragraphs fail.
- Quote-posts and replies can strip context—each post should remain accurate alone.
- Links compete with in-thread reading; key limits must appear in-thread, not only behind the link.
- Algorithm favors engagement, but editorial trust must not be traded for bait.

---

## 4. Content Goals

- One verified source → one thread or standalone post with a single teaching arc.
- Teach distinctions (e.g., sterile saline vs. DIY mix) before product detail.
- State limitations in-thread before the final CTA.
- Link to canonical PiercingConnect article for tables, alternatives, and source notes.

---

## 5. Tone of Voice

Direct, precise, calm. Short sentences. No hashtag spam. No dunking on other creators. Correct misinformation politely with sourced boundaries, not mockery.

---

## 6. Opening / Hook Rules

- Post 1 must deliver standalone value: a accurate distinction, scope statement, or reader question answered in one line.
- Do not thread-start with “🧵 A thread on why you NEED…”
- Avoid “Unpopular opinion:” unless the claim is verifiable and sourced.
- No engagement bait (“Like if you’ve been pierced,” “RT for good luck healing”).

---

## 7. Content Structure

**Standard thread (5–8 posts):**
1. Hook + scope (what this thread covers / does not promise).
2. Context or category definition.
3. Core fact(s) from verified source.
4. Professional guidance vs. manufacturer information (explicit separation).
5. Limitations and individual variation.
6. When to seek professional guidance.
7. CTA + link to full PiercingConnect review + disclosure if link earns commission.

**Standalone posts:** One claim + one limit + link optional.

Number threads (1/n) for clarity. Each post under character limit with room for readability.

---

## 8. CTA Strategy

- Final post: link to full review with “formula, limits, alternatives—not a sales pitch.”
- Do not split affiliate link across posts to hide disclosure.
- Pin the thread on profile only if evergreen and re-verified periodically.
- Replies to questions: answer from source or defer to full article; do not diagnose.

---

## 9. Evergreen Strategy

Threads should avoid date stamps and trending hashtags unless covering verified news.

- Build a pinned evergreen thread library (aftercare basics, evidence boundaries).
- When source updates, reply to the original thread with a correction post or publish a new numbered thread and unpin the outdated one.
- Quote-post evergreen threads for news only when adding verified context, not hot takes.

---

## 10. News / Trend Strategy

Timely posts only for verified changes in source record (formulation, guidance, safety).

- Post 1 states the news; later posts separate fact from editorial analysis.
- After news fades, add a reply linking to an evergreen thread or article section that captures the durable lesson.
- Do not live-tweet unverified piercing incidents or pile-on controversies.
- Trending topics unrelated to piercing safety are out of scope.

---

## 11. Affiliate Integration Rules

- Disclosure in the same post as the affiliate link or the post immediately before it.
- Never imply commission links are “official medical resources.”
- Product mention threads must include limitations before the link post.
- Editorial independence stated plainly once per thread when commercial links appear.

---

## 12. Things to Avoid

- Threads that are article paragraphs split arbitrarily mid-sentence.
- Diagnostic replies (“that’s a keloid”).
- Fear hooks (“STOP using X immediately”).
- Hashtag stuffing (#piercing #aftercare #healing #infection…).
- Outrage quote-posts of other creators.
- Deleting posts that contained errors without a correction post.
- “Ratio” engagement tactics on safety topics.

---

## 13. Success Metrics

- Profile link clicks to PiercingConnect canonical URLs.
- Thread completion proxy: replies asking informed follow-ups vs. confusion.
- Quote-post accuracy (are others misrepresenting our claims?).
- Follower growth steady, not spike-and-crash from bait.
- Correction frequency (lower is better).
- Bookmarks on evergreen threads (reference intent).

---

## 14. Approval Checklist

- [ ] Thread derived from one verified source; each post accurate if read alone.
- [ ] Hook is non-sensational and scoped.
- [ ] Guidance vs. manufacturer vs. evidence limits separated in-thread.
- [ ] Limitations appear before final CTA/link.
- [ ] Escalation guidance included for health-adjacent threads.
- [ ] No guaranteed outcomes or engagement bait.
- [ ] Affiliate disclosure adjacent to commercial links.
- [ ] CTA points to full PiercingConnect review.
- [ ] Evergreen threads free of stale news framing.
- [ ] Human editorial approval before publish.
