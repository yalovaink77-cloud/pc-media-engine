# PiercingConnect Evergreen Content Master Plan

**Version:** 1.0  
**Status:** Editorial Strategy Document  
**Scope:** Long-term evergreen content architecture for PiercingConnect

This document defines the durable content architecture that will serve as PiercingConnect’s primary organic traffic engine. It is subordinate to the PiercingConnect Editorial Style Guide and Platform Editorial Playbooks. It is not an implementation specification, workflow manual, or automation prompt.

---

## 1. Mission

PiercingConnect’s evergreen mission is to help people make informed, safe decisions about body piercing by publishing accurate, problem-led educational content that remains useful months and years after publication.

Evergreen content exists to:

- Answer real questions people search before, during, and after a piercing.
- Build trust through calm, evidence-bound guidance—not hype or conversion pressure.
- Create a canonical knowledge layer that platform-specific formats can summarize without distorting facts.
- Support sustainable organic traffic growth through depth, accuracy, and internal coherence—not trend chasing.

Every evergreen asset must leave the reader better equipped to protect their health and know when to seek qualified professional help.

---

## 2. Content Pillars

Evergreen architecture rests on five pillars. Each pillar is problem-led; products appear only where they genuinely help answer a user problem.

| Pillar | Purpose | Primary intent |
|--------|---------|----------------|
| **Aftercare Foundations** | Core routines, terminology, and safe boundaries | “What should I do?” |
| **Healing & Complications** | Symptom context, likely causes, escalation | “Is this normal?” |
| **Product Education** | Category literacy, formula, fit—not hype | “What kind of product fits?” |
| **Professional Guidance** | When and how to involve piercers and clinicians | “Who should I ask?” |
| **Decision Support** | Comparisons, alternatives, informed choice | “What are my options?” |

Pillar balance target (by article count over time): roughly 40% Aftercare + Healing, 25% Product Education, 20% Decision Support, 15% Professional Guidance cross-cutting content. Commercial depth must never exceed educational depth within any pillar.

## 3. Problem Clusters

Problem clusters are the primary organizing unit for evergreen traffic. Each cluster maps a persistent user problem to a hub page and supporting spokes.

### Tier 1 — Core (launch priority)

| Cluster | Hub topic | Example user problems |
|---------|-----------|----------------------|
| **New piercing aftercare** | Sterile saline and daily rinse basics | What should I clean with? How often? Can I use salt water? |
| **Early healing signs** | Normal vs. concerning symptoms | Is redness normal? What is crust vs. pus? |
| **Saline category literacy** | Packaged sterile saline explained | Spray vs. soak? Isotonic vs. hypertonic? DIY risks? |
| **When to get help** | Escalation and professional boundaries | When is swelling too much? Piercer vs. doctor? |

### Tier 2 — Growth

| Cluster | Hub topic | Example user problems |
|---------|-----------|----------------------|
| **Location-specific care** | Ear, nose, oral, body aftercare differences | Why does oral healing differ? Can I use the same spray everywhere? |
| **Jewelry & irritation** | Material, fit, migration context | Is my jewelry causing this bump? |
| **Bump & irritation explainers** | Irritation vs. granuloma vs. keloid (with diagnostic limits) | What is this bump? (informational only) |
| **Aftercare mistakes** | Common errors and safe corrections | Am I over-cleaning? Should I rotate jewelry? |

### Tier 3 — Authority depth

| Cluster | Hub topic | Example user problems |
|---------|-----------|----------------------|
| **Sensitive skin & allergies** | Reaction context without universal claims | Hypoallergenic—what does it mean? |
| **Healed vs. healing product use** | Stage-appropriate product boundaries | Can I use this on a fresh piercing? |
| **Long-healing piercings** | Cartilage, oral, surface—timeline realism | Why is my cartilage piercing still sore? |

Each cluster requires one **hub article** (comprehensive, canonical) and three to eight **spoke articles** (specific questions). Hubs link down; spokes link up.

## 4. Product Clusters

Product content is organized by **category first**, product second. Individual product reviews are spokes; category hubs are the traffic anchors.

### Category hubs (evergreen)

- Sterile saline sprays (packaged, isotonic, preservative-free context)
- Sterile saline washes and soaks
- Piercing aftercare kits (category critique, not kit hype)
- Jewelry materials for healing (informational, not vendor-led)

### Product review spokes

- One canonical review per product with verified source record.
- Reviews link to their category hub and relevant problem clusters (e.g., new piercing aftercare, saline literacy).
- Alternatives sections remain neutral; no default “winner” across the catalog.

### Product cluster rules

- No product review without a identifiable user problem it addresses.
- Every product page must state who it may suit, who should not assume it, and when to seek professional guidance.
- Product clusters feed commercial traffic only after the reader understands the category and limits.

## 5. Educational Clusters

Educational clusters teach durable concepts that outlive any single product or trend.

| Cluster | Focus | Evergreen value |
|---------|-------|-----------------|
| **Terminology** | Isotonic saline, sterile, migration, irritation | Reduces misinformation across all content |
| **Evidence literacy** | Manufacturer vs. APP guidance vs. clinical proof | Builds trust; limits overclaiming |
| **Aftercare philosophy** | Gentle care, avoid over-intervention | Counteracts harmful social media advice |
| **Anatomy & healing stages** | Fresh, early, active, healed—defined consistently | Supports location and product boundaries |
| **Myth correction** | DIY sea salt, harsh antiseptics, rotation myths | High search volume; high trust payoff |

Educational articles should be written to be **platform-neutral**: the same verified claims may appear in a WordPress guide, a carousel, or a thread without rewriting the underlying facts.

---

## 6. Commercial Clusters

Commercial clusters connect education to informed purchase consideration—not the reverse.

| Cluster type | Role | Boundaries |
|--------------|------|------------|
| **Category comparisons** | Neutral peer comparison within sterile saline, etc. | Declared criteria; no commission-driven ranking |
| **“Best for” decision guides** | Situation-based fit (first piercing, sensitive skin caution) | “May suit”—never universal suitability |
| **Budget & availability context** | Format and size without price invention | No fabricated prices or fake scarcity |
| **Affiliate transparency hub** | How PiercingConnect reviews and discloses | Editorial independence stated plainly |

Commercial clusters must always link to limitations content and escalation guidance. A reader should never reach a product comparison without passing through category education first (via internal links or site architecture).

## 7. Seasonal Content

Seasonal content is **secondary** to evergreen hubs. When used, it must teach something durable—not exploit a calendar moment.

### Permitted seasonal angles

- **Summer:** Aftercare and swimming boundaries (what guidance says, what remains uncertain).
- **Back-to-school / holidays:** Gift-giving and “new piercing” preparation—always with professional guidance emphasis.
- **Winter:** Dry skin and irritation context (not product panic).

### Seasonal rules

- Seasonal posts must link to evergreen hubs; they do not replace them.
- No seasonal URL unless the content would be misleading without the date context.
- Seasonal social posts may be ephemeral; canonical WordPress pages remain evergreen.
- After the season, extract durable teaching into evergreen articles; archive or noindex thin seasonal pages if they add no lasting value.

## 8. News Strategy

News is a **feeder**, not the traffic engine. Timely content exists to capture verified change and route readers to updated evergreen assets.

### Publish news when

- Product formulation or labeling changes (verified source record).
- Professional association guidance updates (APP-aligned or equivalent).
- Safety advisories affecting aftercare categories PiercingConnect covers.
- Material corrections to prior published claims.

### News workflow (editorial, not technical)

1. Publish a concise update post stating what changed and what did not.
2. Update the relevant evergreen hub and product review in place.
3. Add a cross-link from news post to evergreen canonical URL.
4. After 60–90 days, merge durable lessons into the evergreen page; keep news post only if historically useful.

### Do not publish news for

- Unverified viral piercing incidents.
- Celebrity piercing trends without educational value.
- Speculative “new miracle product” cycles without source records.
- Algorithm-driven hot takes.

## 9. Platform Adaptation Strategy

One verified knowledge source → many platform-native formats. WordPress holds the canonical record; other platforms summarize.

| Platform | Evergreen role | Adaptation principle |
|----------|----------------|----------------------|
| **WordPress** | Canonical hub and review | Full evidence, sources, disclosure |
| **Instagram** | Carousels and Reels from hub sections | One idea per slide; limits on final slide |
| **YouTube** | Shorts from hooks; long-form from hub structure | Teach → limit → CTA to canonical URL |
| **Facebook** | Readable summaries | Self-contained post + link |
| **X** | Threads from hub sections | Each post accurate alone |
| **Pinterest** | Search-intent pins from hub titles | Description stands alone |

Adaptation rules:

- Platform playbooks govern format; evergreen master plan governs *what* gets adapted.
- Never adapt commercial CTAs without adapting limitations in the same asset.
- Platform content must link to the same canonical URL per topic.
- Automation-ready rule: each hub section should be extractable as a discrete, self-contained claim block with its evidence tier labeled internally.

## 10. Internal Linking Strategy

Internal linking is the structural engine of evergreen SEO and reader utility.

### Link hierarchy

```
Problem hub
  ├── Spoke: specific question
  ├── Spoke: related symptom
  ├── Category hub (product type)
  │     └── Product review
  └── Escalation / professional guidance article
```

### Linking rules

- Every product review links upward to a category hub and sideways to at least one problem cluster.
- Every spoke links to its hub and to escalation content if health-adjacent.
- Comparison articles link to each product’s canonical review, not duplicate product summaries.
- Anchor text describes the destination topic honestly—no keyword-stuffed anchors.
- Orphan pages (no inbound internal links) are publication blockers.
- Maximum practical depth: three clicks from homepage to any spoke.

### Pillar cross-links

- Aftercare Foundations ↔ Healing & Complications (normal vs. concerning).
- Product Education ↔ Decision Support (category → comparison).
- All commercial pages ↔ Professional Guidance (escalation triggers).

## 11. Affiliate Integration Philosophy

Affiliate revenue supports the operation; it does not steer the architecture.

**Principles:**

- Education first: readers encounter problem context and category literacy before product recommendations.
- Independence: commission availability does not determine inclusion, ranking, or verdict.
- Transparency: disclosure on every page and adapted asset with affiliate links.
- Honest limits: affiliate products receive the same limitation language as non-affiliate peers.
- Neutral comparisons: criteria declared before results; ties and qualified recommendations are valid outcomes.
- No urgency: evergreen content never uses scarcity, countdown, or “buy now to heal faster” framing.

Affiliate integration is a **layer** applied to decision-support content—not a separate content type that bypasses editorial standards.

## 12. Content Lifecycle

Every evergreen asset moves through defined lifecycle stages.

| Stage | Definition | Exit criteria |
|-------|------------|---------------|
| **Planned** | Mapped to cluster; user problem documented | Problem statement and hub relationship approved |
| **Drafted** | Written from verified sources | Passes editorial checklist |
| **In review** | Human editorial and fact-check | Required professional review for health-adjacent content |
| **Published** | Live canonical URL | Disclosure, sources, internal links complete |
| **Distributed** | Platform adaptations live | Adaptations link to canonical URL; limits preserved |
| **Maintained** | On review schedule | Source records still valid |
| **Updated** | Material revision | Change logged; adaptations flagged for refresh |
| **Consolidated** | Merged into hub or redirected | No duplicate topic URLs |
| **Retired** | Removed or archived | Inaccurate beyond repair; redirect to successor |

Lifecycle ownership: editorial team assigns a **content owner** per hub for maintenance accountability.

## 13. Update Policy

Evergreen traffic depends on accuracy over time. Updates are editorial duty, not optional housekeeping.

### Trigger an update when

- Product formulation, sizing, or labeling changes (verified).
- Professional guidance relevant to the topic changes.
- Internal editorial standards evolve in ways that affect published claims.
- A material factual error is identified.
- Internal links break or point to retired pages.
- Platform adaptations contradict the updated canonical page.

### Update procedure (editorial)

1. Revise the canonical WordPress page in place.
2. Note significant corrections publicly when reader decisions could have been affected.
3. Refresh source notes and disclosure if commercial relationships changed.
4. Flag platform adaptations for aligned refresh.
5. Do not change publish date deceptively; use a visible “updated” note when changes are substantive.

### Review cadence

| Content type | Minimum review interval |
|--------------|-------------------------|
| Product reviews | Every 6 months |
| Category hubs | Every 6 months |
| Aftercare / healing hubs | Every 12 months |
| Terminology / evidence literacy | Every 12 months |
| Comparison articles | When any compared product updates |

Stale content with outdated product or guidance claims must be updated, consolidated, or retired—not left indexed.

## 14. Success Metrics

Measure long-term organic health and reader trust—not short-term spikes alone.

### Primary metrics (evergreen engine)

- **Organic sessions** to hub and spoke pages (trend over 6–12 months).
- **Non-branded search impressions** for problem-cluster queries.
- **Click-through rate** from search (title/description honesty signal).
- **Internal link traversal:** spoke → hub → related cluster (reader depth).
- **Return visitor rate** to educational content.

### Trust and quality metrics

- **Correction frequency** (material errors per quarter—minimize).
- **Escalation content engagement** (readers reaching “when to seek help” sections).
- **Time on page** on limitations and FAQ sections—not only introductions.
- **Downstream professional-guidance section views** from product pages.

### Commercial metrics (secondary, bounded)

- **Affiliate click-through** only evaluated alongside educational engagement ratios.
- **Comparison page exits** to multiple product reviews (informed browsing)—not single-product funnel alone.
- **Disclosure visibility audits** (zero pages with affiliate links and missing disclosure).

### Platform metrics (supporting)

- Saves, bookmarks, and canonical link clicks from social adaptations.
- Pin and video completion rates through limitations/CTA segments.

### Anti-metrics (watch for decline)

- Traffic concentrated on thin or outdated pages.
- Rising bounce rate on product pages without hub referrals.
- Increasing reliance on news/trend posts vs. evergreen hub growth.
- Frequent post-publish corrections on the same cluster (signals architecture or sourcing weakness).
