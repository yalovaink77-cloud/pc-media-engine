# PiercingConnect Revenue Roadmap v1

**Version:** 1.0  
**Status:** Operational Strategy Document  
**Scope:** First-phase roadmap from trusted education to sustainable affiliate revenue

This document defines how PiercingConnect converts editorial trust into measurable, sustainable affiliate revenue. It is subordinate to the Editorial Style Guide, Evergreen Content Master Plan, and Platform Editorial Playbooks. It is not an implementation specification, technical workflow, or automation prompt.

---

## 1. Mission

PiercingConnect’s revenue mission is to earn affiliate income only as a consequence of helping readers make informed, safe piercing decisions—never by compromising editorial independence, evidence boundaries, or reader safety.

Revenue exists to sustain high-quality educational publishing. Trust is the asset; affiliate clicks are the outcome of useful guidance, not the purpose of it.

---

## 2. Revenue Philosophy

| Principle | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| **Trust before revenue** | No commission, deadline, or traffic target justifies weak sourcing, missing disclosures, or exaggerated claims. |
| **Educational before commercial** | Readers encounter problem context and category literacy before product recommendations. |
| **Evergreen is the engine** | Long-lived hub and spoke articles compound organic traffic; news and social amplify them. |
| **Problem-first architecture** | Content is planned around user problems, not affiliate catalog availability. |
| **Measured purpose** | Every asset has a defined business role: discover, educate, compare, convert, retain, or maintain trust. |
| **Independence is non-negotiable** | Affiliate relationships do not determine inclusion, ranking, or verdict. |
| **Honest limits sell nothing** | Limitations and escalation guidance remain visible on every commercial path. |

Revenue success is sustainable only when readers would recommend PiercingConnect without knowing affiliate links exist.

Product reviews follow **search demand × category fit × source-record readiness**, not commission rate.

### Tier 2 — Category completion (reviews 6–15)

Remaining sterile saline sprays and washes with verified records; one review per canonical product only.

### Tier 3 — Adjacent categories (reviews 16–30)

Aftercare kits, complementary rinse formats, and stage-appropriate products—only where problem clusters exist first.

### Tier 4 — Long tail (reviews 31+)

Lower-volume products published only when:

- Parent category hub is live.
- At least three internal links from problem content exist.
- Source record is complete and current.

**Exclusion criteria:** No review without verified product record, limitation section, alternatives mention, affiliate disclosure, and link to category hub.

## 6. Problem-First Content Strategy

Every content decision starts with a user problem statement—not a product SKU or keyword spreadsheet.

### Planning sequence

1. **Problem:** What is the reader trying to decide or understand?
2. **Cluster:** Which hub owns this problem long-term?
3. **Evidence:** What verified sources support an answer?
4. **Format:** Hub, spoke, review, comparison, or guide?
5. **Commercial relevance:** Does a product category help—or should this stay non-commercial?
6. **Business purpose:** Discover, educate, compare, convert, retain, or maintain?
7. **Platform derivatives:** Which adaptations serve discovery without replacing the canonical article?

### Problem-first rules

- If the problem can be answered without a product, publish education first.
- Product mentions appear only where they reduce reader uncertainty—not to fill word count.
- Comparison content requires declared criteria before any product names appear.
- High-anxiety problems (infection signs, bumps) require escalation guidance in the same asset.

## 7. Multi-Platform Publishing Workflow

One verified canonical article → controlled platform adaptation. Social drives readers **back** to authoritative WordPress content.

### Workflow stages

| Stage | Owner | Output |
|-------|-------|--------|
| **1. Canonical publish** | Editorial | Approved WordPress article (source of truth) |
| **2. Adaptation brief** | Editorial | Section-level extract list with evidence boundaries flagged |
| **3. Platform draft** | Editorial / contributor | Native format per platform playbook |
| **4. Platform review** | Editorial | Accuracy, limits, disclosure, CTA check |
| **5. Distribute** | Operations | Publish adaptations with canonical link |
| **6. Monitor** | Editorial | Track clicks to canonical URL; flag drift |

### Platform priority by phase

| Phase | WordPress | Instagram | Pinterest | YouTube Shorts | Facebook | X |
|-------|-----------|-----------|-----------|----------------|----------|---|
| Launch | Required | Carousels | Pins | Optional | Summaries | Threads |
| Growth | Required | Reels + carousels | Board expansion | Shorts library | Page rhythm | Thread library |
| Maturity | Updated first | Refresh on hub update | SEO pin refresh | Evergreen cuts | Reshare evergreen | Pin thread updates |

**Rule:** No platform adaptation publishes before its canonical article is approved. No adaptation introduces claims absent from the source.

## 8. Affiliate Integration Workflow

Affiliate integration is a governed layer applied after editorial approval—not a parallel publishing track.

### Integration stages

1. **Eligibility check:** Product meets review standard; limitations documented; source record current.
2. **Link placement:** After educational sections; adjacent to disclosure on WordPress; same post/thread as disclosure on social.
3. **Disclosure:** Plain language on every commission-bearing destination and adaptation that links to it.
4. **Independence confirmation:** Verdict and ranking unchanged by commission availability.
5. **Comparison neutrality:** Criteria declared; no arbitrary ordering for affiliate preference.
6. **Post-publish audit:** Monthly spot-check for missing disclosures or contradictory suitability language.

### Commercial asset types and affiliate role

| Asset type | Affiliate role |
|------------|----------------|
| Problem hub | None (link to category hub only) |
| Category hub | Optional category-level links after education |
| Product review | Product-specific links after limitations |
| Comparison | Links per product; neutral presentation |
| Social adaptation | Link to canonical review page (disclosed) |

## 9. Internal Linking Strategy

Internal linking converts isolated articles into a compounding traffic and trust engine.

### Revenue-aware linking model

```
Problem hub (no affiliate)
    → Category hub (light commercial context)
        → Product review (convert)
            → Comparison + alternatives (compare)
    → Escalation article (trust)
    → FAQ spoke (retain)
```

### Linking requirements at launch

- Every product review: minimum 3 inbound links from non-commercial content before paid distribution.
- Every comparison: links to each product’s canonical review.
- Every hub: links to all live spokes; spokes link back to hub.
- Every commercial page: link to escalation / professional guidance content.

### Ongoing linking rhythm

- Monthly: identify orphan pages and add inbound links.
- Quarterly: audit anchor text for accuracy and topic honesty.
- On update: refresh broken links and add links from new spokes to existing hubs.

## 10. Content Update Cycle

Stale content erodes trust and revenue. Updates are operational duty.

| Content type | Review cadence | Revenue trigger to prioritize update |
|--------------|----------------|--------------------------------------|
| Tier 1 product reviews | Every 6 months | CTR decline > 20% quarter-over-quarter |
| Category hubs | Every 6 months | New product reviews added to category |
| Problem hubs | Every 12 months | Rising bounce rate from search |
| Comparisons | On any compared product change | Affiliate click imbalance suggesting confusion |
| Platform adaptations | Within 14 days of canonical update | Canonical material change |
| News posts | 60–90 days post-publish | Merge into evergreen or archive |

**Update priority order:** factual safety errors first → formulation changes → guidance changes → commercial freshness → SEO cosmetic updates.

Retire or consolidate pages that cannot be maintained accurately; redirect to successor canonical URLs.

## 12. Human Approval Workflow

No PiercingConnect content enters public distribution without human editorial approval. Revenue targets do not bypass this gate.

### Approval stages

| Stage | Applies to | Approver |
|-------|------------|----------|
| **Cluster fit** | New content proposals | Editorial lead |
| **Draft review** | Canonical articles | Editor |
| **Fact-check** | All factual claims | Editor / fact-checker |
| **Safety review** | Health-adjacent content | Qualified reviewer |
| **Commercial review** | Reviews, comparisons, affiliate-bearing pages | Editor + commercial independence check |
| **Platform review** | Social, video, pin adaptations | Editor |
| **Final approval** | Publish and distribute | Authorized editorial approver |

### Outcomes

- **Approved** — publish and distribute per workflow.
- **Revise** — specific fixes required; re-review mandatory.
- **Reject** — does not meet standards; no publish.

### Revenue-specific approval checks

- Limitations section present and aligned with advantages.
- Affiliate disclosure visible and plain.
- Product verdict independent of commission.
- Comparison criteria declared before results.
- Canonical URL assigned before platform adaptations.

## 13. Monthly Operating Rhythm

A repeating monthly cadence balances publishing, maintenance, and measurement.

### Week 1 — Plan and audit

- Review prior month KPIs against phase targets.
- Select 4–8 canonical articles for the month (problem-first queue).
- Run disclosure and orphan-page audit.
- Identify product reviews due for scheduled refresh.

### Week 2 — Produce canonical content

- Draft and review new hub, spoke, or review content.
- Complete safety and commercial approval gates.
- Publish approved WordPress articles only.

### Week 3 — Adapt and distribute

- Create platform adaptations from approved canonical sources.
- Review adaptations against platform playbooks.
- Publish social, pin, and video derivatives with canonical links.

### Week 4 — Maintain and learn

- Execute scheduled content updates and comparison refreshes.
- Merge or archive eligible news into evergreen where applicable.
- Document KPI deltas, corrections, and next-month priorities.
- Editorial retrospective: what problems drove traffic and revenue?

### Standing monthly outputs

- Published canonical count vs. roadmap pace.
- KPI dashboard summary (traffic, CTR, affiliate clicks, conversions).
- Update log (what changed and why).
- Approval log (who approved what).
