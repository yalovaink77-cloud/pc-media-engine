## Editorial Summary

Recovery Sterile Saline Wash **7.4 oz** (SKU **MED-637**) is a packaged sterile saline spray sold for piercing aftercare. Official product copy describes it as **preservative-free**, **drug-free**, **vegan**, **CFC-free**, and without additives, with **bag-on-valve** technology, a **360°** spray dispenser, and production in a **cGMP** American facility. (`official-recovery-aftercare`)

This review evaluates the **7.4 oz** SKU against **declared criteria**—documentation quality (SDS / CoA / gamma links), concentration evidence, sterility and formulation marketing claims, directions/warnings, and overlap or gaps versus Association of Professional Piercers (APP) aftercare guidance. It is **not** a ranked “best saline” verdict and does **not** guarantee healing outcomes.

**Important evidence boundary:** Official **7.4 oz SDS** Section 1 lists the product synonym **“Sterile Saline Wound Wash 0.9%.”** That is the verified concentration evidence used here. Do **not** treat it as a quantitative water/NaCl ingredient table from SDS Section 3, and do **not** cite CoA body text for composition (not verified). Do **not** apply the separate Purified Saline Wash 1.5 oz / 4 oz MSDS path as if it were the 7.4 oz Sterile Saline Wash document. (`official-recovery-aftercare`)

For category literacy, start with the [Sterile Saline Complete Guide](#related-guides) (`pc-hub-sterile-saline-complete-guide`).

This article is informational, not medical advice.

## Evidence and Guideline Alignment

### Professional guidance (category layer) — `app-aftercare`

APP Suggested Aftercare for Body Piercings strongly encourages **sterile saline labeled for wound wash**, with ingredients listing **0.9% sodium chloride** as the only ingredient (sometimes purified water). APP advises against DIY sea-salt mixes, harsh cleaners, over-cleaning, and using contact-lens saline, nasal spray, or eye drops. APP does **not** endorse any specific brand. (`app-aftercare`)

## FAQ

**Q: Does Recovery Sterile Saline Wash 7.4 oz contain 0.9% sodium chloride?**  
A: Official **7.4 oz SDS** Section 1 lists the synonym **“Sterile Saline Wound Wash 0.9%.”** This review does **not** claim a quantitative Section 3 water/NaCl percentage table or verified CoA composition text. (`official-recovery-aftercare`)

**Q: How often should I use it?**  
A: Official instructions: as often as needed **or as directed by your piercer**. APP guidance does not give a fixed universal daily schedule for all piercings. (`official-recovery-aftercare`, `app-aftercare`)

**Q: Is the Purified Saline Wash MSDS the same document?**  
A: No. Resources-page Purified Saline Wash MSDS covers **1.5 oz / 4 oz** sizing and is a **separate** path from the **7.4 oz Sterile Saline Wash** SDS linked on the MED-637 product page. (`official-recovery-aftercare`)

**Q: Is it APP-endorsed?**  
A: No. APP guidance is formulation-based and does not endorse brands. SDS wound-wash / 0.9% synonym is manufacturer documentation—not APP approval. (`app-aftercare`)

**Q: Can I use homemade salt water instead?**  
A: APP suggested aftercare no longer recommends DIY sea-salt mixes; they are commonly too salty and may over-dry the piercing. Prefer packaged sterile saline wound wash where available. (`app-aftercare`)

**Q: What if I have irritation or worry about infection?**  
A: Do not self-diagnose from an article. Contact a piercer or healthcare provider for concerning or worsening symptoms. Follow product IFU to discontinue and seek care if irritation develops.
