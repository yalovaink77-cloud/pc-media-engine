# YouTube Editorial Playbook

**Version:** 1.0  
**Status:** Platform Editorial Standard  
**Scope:** PiercingConnect content adapted for YouTube (long-form, Shorts, Community posts)

This playbook defines how verified PiercingConnect knowledge is transformed into YouTube-native content while preserving editorial integrity. It is subordinate to the PiercingConnect Editorial Style Guide.

---

## 1. Platform Purpose

YouTube is a search-driven education and demonstration channel. PiercingConnect uses it to explain piercing aftercare, product context, and safety boundaries in depth-friendly formats—Shorts for discovery, long-form for structured learning.

---

## 2. Audience Intent

Viewers search for “how to,” “what is,” and “is this normal” questions. They want clear explanations they can follow at their own pace. Some arrive skeptical of marketing; others want confirmation against professional guidance.

---

## 3. User Behavior

- Reads titles and thumbnails before clicking; abandons if the opening feels promotional or misleading.
- Uses chapters and timestamps on longer videos.
- Shorts are consumed in sequence; retention drops if the hook overpromises.
- Comments ask follow-up questions; pinned comments shape trust.
- Subscribes when content consistently respects limits and cites guidance honestly.

---

## 4. Content Goals

- One verified source → one focused video or Short with a single learning outcome.
- Explain *why* before *what to buy*.
- Separate product information, professional guidance, and evidence limits on camera and in description.
- Drive viewers to the canonical PiercingConnect article for tables, comparisons, and source notes.
- Build a library of evergreen explainers that remain accurate over time.

---

## 5. Tone of Voice

Measured, conversational expertise. Speak directly to the viewer (“you”) without condescension. Prefer “may,” “according to manufacturer materials,” and “professional guidance recommends” over certainty. Never perform alarm or hype for retention.

---

## 6. Opening / Hook Rules

**Long-form (first 30 seconds):**
- State what the viewer will learn and who it is for.
- Name the limit upfront if health-adjacent (“This is not medical advice”).
- Do not open with a product unboxing or affiliate link.

**Shorts (first 3 seconds):**
- One accurate tension or distinction on screen and in VO.
- No clickbait titles that the video body cannot support.

Titles must match content. Thumbnails may highlight a concept, not a false outcome.

---

## 7. Content Structure

**Long-form review/explainer:**
1. Hook + scope (what this covers / does not cover).
2. Context (why sterile saline, aftercare category, etc.).
3. Product or topic detail from verified source.
4. Professional guidance alignment.
5. Advantages (with *why they matter*).
6. Limitations and uncertainties.
7. Who may consider / who should seek professional guidance.
8. CTA to full PiercingConnect article + description links.

**Shorts (45–60s):** Hook → one teaching block → limits → CTA.

**Description:** Summary, chapter timestamps, affiliate disclosure, link to full review, “informational not medical advice” where relevant.

---

## 8. CTA Strategy

- End screen and description: “Read the full PiercingConnect review for formula, evidence, and alternatives.”
- Subscribe CTA is secondary to educational completion.
- No “link below to heal faster” framing.
- Pin a comment with disclosure and link to canonical article when affiliate pages are linked.

---

## 9. Evergreen Strategy

Prioritize videos that answer persistent search intent: aftercare basics, sterile saline explained, product category comparisons, when to see a piercer.

- Avoid dating titles (“2026 best spray”) unless the source is explicitly time-bound and maintained.
- Update description and pinned comment when source articles are revised; add a note in description for material changes.
- Shorts cut from evergreen long-form must remain valid if watched alone.
- Playlists group by topic (aftercare, product reviews, safety)—not by publish week alone.

---

## 10. News / Trend Strategy

Publish timely videos only for verified changes: formulation updates, recalled products, revised APP guidance, or major safety advisories in the source record.

- Title must state the news; body must separate fact from speculation.
- After 30–90 days, extract durable teaching into an evergreen video and link both ways in description.
- Do not react to viral piercing mishaps with diagnostic or shaming content.
- Trend-jacking formats (e.g., arbitrary challenge templates) are prohibited for health topics.

---

## 11. Affiliate Integration Rules

- Verbal and written disclosure in first 30 seconds or description top: commission may be earned; editorial independence maintained.
- Affiliate links live in description, not burned into the first frame as the primary message.
- Product verdicts in video must match written review; limitations must appear on screen or in VO.
- Comparison videos do not rank products for commission; present category peers neutrally per editorial standard.

---

## 12. Things to Avoid

- Misleading thumbnails (infection imagery, exaggerated redness).
- Guaranteed healing timelines or “doctor reacts” framing without qualified review.
- Reading the full article verbatim on camera.
- Keyword-stuffed titles unrelated to video content.
- Omitting limitations to improve retention.
- Superlatives unsupported by source (“best on the market”).
- Comment moderation that deletes legitimate safety questions.

---

## 13. Success Metrics

- Watch time and average view duration (especially past the limitations section).
- Click-through to PiercingConnect canonical URLs from description.
- Search impressions and CTR for evergreen queries (quality of title/thumbnail match).
- Subscriber growth correlated with educational series, not single viral Shorts.
- Ratio of videos requiring post-publish correction (minimize).
- Qualitative comment sentiment: questions answered, misinformation corrected politely.

---

## 14. Approval Checklist

- [ ] Script derived from verified source; no new claims, prices, or links invented.
- [ ] Title, thumbnail, and opening 30s are aligned and non-sensational.
- [ ] Manufacturer vs. guidance vs. evidence limits distinguished in script.
- [ ] Health-adjacent content includes escalation triggers and not-medical-advice framing.
- [ ] Limitations section present; no guaranteed outcomes.
- [ ] Affiliate disclosure in description and verbally where required.
- [ ] CTA points to full PiercingConnect article.
- [ ] Evergreen videos free of stale trend/date framing.
- [ ] News videos flagged for sunset or merge into evergreen follow-up.
- [ ] Human editorial approval before publish.
