# Pinterest Editorial Playbook

**Version:** 1.0  
**Status:** Platform Editorial Standard  
**Scope:** PiercingConnect content adapted for Pinterest (pins, boards, descriptions)

This playbook defines how verified PiercingConnect knowledge is transformed into Pinterest-native content while preserving editorial integrity. It is subordinate to the PiercingConnect Editorial Style Guide.

---

## 1. Platform Purpose

Pinterest is a visual search and planning channel. PiercingConnect uses it to help people discover and save educational aftercare and product-context resources—optimized for search intent, not impulse buying.

---

## 2. Audience Intent

Users search before acting: “piercing aftercare saline,” “NeilMed piercing spray,” “sterile saline vs salt water.” They want scannable titles, trustworthy descriptions, and content worth saving for later—not flashy ads.

---

## 3. User Behavior

- Discovers via search and related pins; reads title and description before clicking.
- Saves pins to boards for reference; may never click through immediately.
- Expects vertical or clear graphic pins with readable text overlay.
- Trusts pins that look educational, not coupon-like.
- Returns months later; evergreen accuracy is critical.

---

## 4. Content Goals

- One verified source → one pin concept with SEO-friendly title and concise description.
- Match real search queries without keyword stuffing.
- Description must stand alone if the image is never clicked.
- Link to canonical PiercingConnect review or guide.
- Build boards by durable topic (aftercare, sterile saline, product reviews)—not by publish date alone.

---

## 5. Tone of Voice

Informative, neutral, helpful. Write descriptions like a catalog librarian for aftercare—not a marketer. Plain language; define terms once when needed (e.g., isotonic saline).

---

## 6. Opening / Hook Rules

**Pin title:** Lead with the search intent or learning outcome (“Sterile Saline vs Homemade Salt Water for Piercing Aftercare”).

**First line of description:** Expand the title with scope and limit (“Educational overview—not medical advice”).

- Do not use “BEST,” “#1,” or “GUARANTEED HEAL” in titles.
- Avoid clickbait punctuation (“!!!”, “You won’t believe…”).
- Text on pin graphics: one concept per line; readable on mobile.

---

## 7. Content Structure

**Pin asset:**
- Title (SEO-friendly, accurate, ≤ 100 characters ideal).
- Description (150–500 characters core; may extend with limits and disclosure).
- Alt text describing the educational graphic accessibly.
- Link to canonical PiercingConnect URL.

**Description template:**
1. What the pin teaches.
2. Key verified fact (formula, guidance category, etc.).
3. Limitation or uncertainty line.
4. When to seek professional guidance (if health-adjacent).
5. CTA to full review.
6. Affiliate disclosure if destination page includes commission links.

**Board organization:** Aftercare basics | Product reviews | Safety & escalation | Comparisons (neutral).

---

## 8. CTA Strategy

- “Read the full PiercingConnect review for formula, evidence, and alternatives.”
- Save-oriented language allowed (“Save for aftercare reference”) when educational.
- No “Shop now” as primary CTA unless the linked page is clearly labeled and subordinate to education in the pin copy.
- Same canonical URL per topic; avoid duplicate pins with thin variations.

---

## 9. Evergreen Strategy

Pinterest rewards durable pins. Evergreen is the default mode.

- No year in title unless the source is explicitly maintained annually.
- Refresh pin descriptions when source articles update; do not create new misleading URLs.
- Consolidate overlapping pins; one authoritative pin per topic where possible.
- Seasonal boards only when tied to timeless education (e.g., “new piercing aftercare checklist”).

---

## 10. News / Trend Strategy

Create news pins sparingly—for verified formulation changes, safety advisories, or guidance updates in the source record.

- Title must name the change explicitly.
- Link to updated PiercingConnect article section.
- After 60–90 days, update the evergreen pin description to incorporate the durable lesson and archive or unpin the news-only pin.
- Do not pin trend imagery unrelated to verified piercing education.

---

## 11. Affiliate Integration Rules

- Disclosure in pin description whenever the destination may earn commission.
- Educational description must precede any commercial framing.
- Pin design must not mimic official medical or brand logos.
- Multiple affiliate products: use neutral comparison language per editorial comparative standard; no ranked “winner” graphics.

---

## 12. Things to Avoid

- Misleading infographics with unsupported statistics.
- Before/after healing imagery implying guaranteed results.
- Keyword-stuffed descriptions unreadable to humans.
- Thin duplicate pins for SEO spam.
- Linking to non-canonical or expired URLs.
- Promotional coupon aesthetics on health-education pins.
- Diagnostic language in descriptions or overlay text.

---

## 13. Success Metrics

- Outbound clicks to PiercingConnect canonical URLs.
- Saves per impression (reference value).
- Search impressions for target queries (title/description alignment).
- Long-tail traffic months after publish (evergreen health).
- Pin correction/update rate (minimize).
- Board follow growth on educational categories.

---

## 14. Approval Checklist

- [ ] Pin derived from one verified source; title matches search intent accurately.
- [ ] Description complete without requiring the click.
- [ ] Graphic text readable on mobile; one concept per overlay.
- [ ] Evidence boundaries and limitations in description.
- [ ] Health-adjacent pins include escalation guidance and not-medical-advice framing.
- [ ] No superlatives or guaranteed outcomes.
- [ ] Affiliate disclosure in description when applicable.
- [ ] Links to canonical PiercingConnect URL.
- [ ] Evergreen default confirmed; news pins flagged for merge/update.
- [ ] Human editorial approval before publish.
