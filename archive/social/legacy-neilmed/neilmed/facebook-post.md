# Facebook Post — NeilMed Piercing Aftercare Fine Mist

If you’re sorting out piercing aftercare, “sterile saline” and “homemade salt water” often get mixed together. They’re not the same.

NeilMed Piercing Aftercare Fine Mist is a sterile saline spray intended for fresh, early, and actively healing piercings—including ear, nose, oral, and body sites. The formula is simple: sterile water and 0.9% sodium chloride (isotonic saline). It’s preservative-free, sterile, and drug-free, and comes in 75ml and 177ml sizes.

That formulation lines up with Association of Professional Piercers (APP) guidance favoring packaged sterile saline with 0.9% sodium chloride for wound care—and avoiding hypertonic DIY salt mixes, which may cause irritation. According to manufacturer materials, sterile saline sprays may help maintain cleanliness and moisture; that is product-facing language, not a guarantee of healing speed or results.

Possible practical advantages include a controlled sterile rinse, few ingredients, and a fine mist that can cover the site without direct contact. Limitations matter just as much: healing still depends on piercing location, anatomy, jewelry, and overall health. Outcomes and timelines can’t be predicted, and irritation or delayed healing can still happen.

Unusual redness, swelling, or discharge? Talk with a professional piercer or healthcare provider. This is educational information, not medical advice.
