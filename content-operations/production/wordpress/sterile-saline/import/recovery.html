Recovery Sterile Saline Wash 7.4 oz (SKU MED-637) is a packaged sterile saline spray sold for piercing aftercare. Official product copy describes it as preservative-free, drug-free, vegan, CFC-free, and without additives, with bag-on-valve technology, a 360° spray dispenser, and production in a cGMP American facility.

This review evaluates the 7.4 oz SKU against declared criteria—documentation quality (SDS / CoA / gamma links), concentration evidence, sterility and formulation marketing claims, directions/warnings, and overlap or gaps versus Association of Professional Piercers (APP) aftercare guidance. Evidence and Guideline Alignment

Professional guidance (category layer) — app-aftercare

APP advises against DIY sea-salt mixes, harsh cleaners, over-cleaning, and using contact-lens saline, nasal spray, or eye drops. APP does not endorse any specific brand.

FAQ

Q: Does Recovery Sterile Saline Wash 7.4 oz contain 0.9% sodium chloride?
A: Official 7.4 oz SDS Section 1 lists the synonym “Sterile Saline Wound Wash 0.9%.” This review does not claim a quantitative Section 3 water/NaCl percentage table or verified CoA composition text.

Q: How often should I use it?
A: Official instructions: as often as needed or as directed by your piercer. APP guidance does not give a fixed universal daily schedule for all piercings.

Q: Is the Purified Saline Wash MSDS the same document?
A: No. Resources-page Purified Saline Wash MSDS covers 1.5 oz / 4 oz sizing and is a separate path from the 7.4 oz Sterile Saline Wash SDS linked on the MED-637 product page.

Q: Is it APP-endorsed?
A: No. APP guidance is formulation-based and does not endorse brands. SDS wound-wash / 0.9% synonym is manufacturer documentation—not APP approval.

Q: Can I use homemade salt water instead?
A: APP suggested aftercare no longer recommends DIY sea-salt mixes; they are commonly too salty and may over-dry the piercing. Prefer packaged sterile saline wound wash where available.

Q: What if I have irritation or worry about infection?
A: Do not self-diagnose from an article. Contact a piercer or healthcare provider for concerning or worsening symptoms. Follow product IFU to discontinue and seek care if irritation develops.
