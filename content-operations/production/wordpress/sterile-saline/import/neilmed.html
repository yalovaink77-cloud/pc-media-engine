Editorial Summary

NeilMed Piercing Aftercare Fine Mist (also referenced in NeilMed materials as NeilCleanse Piercing Aftercare) is a preservative-free, vegan, sterile saline fine-mist spray sold for piercing aftercare. Official materials describe ingredients as pharmaceutical-grade water and sodium chloride; the official 177 mL back label lists Deionized Water & Sodium Chloride. Available sizes on official pages consulted include 75 mL (travel / TSA carry-on framing) and 177 mL.

This review evaluates the product against declared criteria—ingredient transparency, format and usability, sterility/preservative claims, label warnings, and how it sits next to Association of Professional Piercers (APP) aftercare guidance. It is not a ranked “best saline” verdict and does not guarantee healing outcomes.

Important evidence boundary: Official piercing-aftercare pages and the 177 mL label consulted for this review do not state 0.9% sodium chloride (or 9 mg/mL). That concentration remains unsupported for this SKU and is not claimed here. For category literacy on why 0.9% matters in professional guidance, start with the Sterile Saline Complete Guide.

This article is informational, not medical advice.

Evidence and Guideline Alignment

Professional guidance (category layer) — app-aftercare

APP Suggested Aftercare for Body Piercings strongly encourages sterile saline labeled for wound wash, with ingredients listing 0.9% sodium chloride as the only ingredient (sometimes purified water). APP advises against DIY sea-salt mixes, harsh cleaners, over-cleaning, and using contact-lens saline, nasal spray, or eye drops. APP does not endorse any specific brand.

  • Preservative-free sterile saline spray positioning.

FAQ

Q: Does NeilMed Piercing Aftercare Fine Mist contain 0.9% sodium chloride?
A: That concentration is not verified on the official piercing-aftercare materials and 177 mL label consulted for this review. Check the physical label of the can you purchase; do not rely on this article to assert 0.9%.

Q: How often should I use it?
A: Official directions say apply as needed and to consult a healthcare professional for continued wound care management. No fixed daily schedule is stated on materials consulted. Follow your piercer and the label.

Q: Is it the same as APP-preferred packaged sterile saline?
A: It overlaps on packaged sterile saline, water + sodium chloride, preservative-free framing, and spray use, but APP ingredient guidance calls for 0.9% sodium chloride on the wound-wash label—and that figure remains unresolved for this NeilMed SKU in the evidence layer. APP does not endorse brands.

Q: Can I use homemade salt water instead?
A: APP suggested aftercare no longer recommends DIY sea-salt mixes; they are commonly too salty and may over-dry the piercing. Prefer packaged sterile saline wound wash where available, and ask a local piercer if packaged saline is hard to find in your region.

Q: What if I have irritation or worry about infection?
A: Do not self-diagnose from an article. Contact a piercer or healthcare provider for concerning or worsening symptoms. See also Infection vs. Irritation (guide forthcoming).

Final Verdict

NeilMed Piercing Aftercare Fine Mist is a credible category example of a packaged sterile saline fine-mist spray with clear official support for water + sodium chloride, preservative-free / vegan / sterile marketing claims, usable mist/bag-on-valve features, and standard external-use warnings.

It is not reviewed here as a verified 0.9% isotonic wound-wash formulation. Readers who require that percentage for APP-aligned shopping should confirm on-pack labeling or consider peers where concentration evidence is documented for a specific SKU (e.g., Steri-Wash 4.25 oz label; Recovery 7.4 oz SDS synonym)—without treating those peers as ranked substitutes.
