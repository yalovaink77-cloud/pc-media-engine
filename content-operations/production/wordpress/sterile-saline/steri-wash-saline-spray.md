# Steri-Wash Saline Spray Review


## Editorial Summary

Steri-Wash Sterile Saline Piercing Spray / Wash is a **sterile saline piercing spray-mist** sold on the official Steri-Wash storefront in **3 oz**, **4.25 oz**, and **8 oz** sizes. Official features list **no drugs or preservatives**, **360° dispensing**, sterility maintained throughout canister life, and spray ingredients as **Water & Sodium Chloride**.

This review evaluates Steri-Wash against **declared criteria**—ingredient transparency, SKU-specific concentration evidence, sterility/preservative claims, format, and overlap or gaps versus Association of Professional Piercers (APP) aftercare guidance. It is **not** a ranked “best saline” verdict and does **not** guarantee healing outcomes.

**Important evidence boundary (SKU-scoped):** Official **4.25 oz** product back label evidence lists **Purified Water** and **0.9% USP NaCl** and describes an **isotonic** solution. That **0.9%** claim is **not** verified for **3 oz** or **8 oz** sizes on materials consulted—do not treat it as a full-line fact. For category literacy, start with the [Sterile Saline Complete Guide](/guides/sterile-saline-for-piercing-aftercare/).

This article is informational, not medical advice.

## Verified Formula

**What is supported (line / spray framing)**

- Official frontpage features: spray ingredients **Water & Sodium Chloride**.  
- Official features: **no drugs or preservatives**.  
- Related Steri-Wash Sterile Saline **Wipes** (separate SKU class): water and sodium chloride with no additional additives listed.  

**What is supported (4.25 oz SKU only)**

- Official **4.25 oz** product back label image: ingredients **Purified Water, 0.9% USP NaCl (sodium chloride)**.  
- Same **4.25 oz** back label: describes Sterile Saline Piercing Wash as an **isotonic** solution with NaCl balance matching the human body (manufacturer label wording).  
- Official 4.25 oz copy: fine mist targeted actuator and gentle cleaning framing.  

**What is explicitly not claimed**

- **0.9% USP NaCl / isotonic concentration across all SKUs** (3 oz, 4.25 oz, and 8 oz) — unsupported; verified concentration is **4.25 oz label only**.  
- **0.9% proven from wholesale page “.09% Sterile Saline” copy alone** — unsupported; use the back label, not the “.09%” marketing string.  
- Healing or infection-prevention outcomes.  
- Suitability for **oral** piercings.  

Readers matching APP ingredient guidance should **confirm the physical label for the size they buy**—especially if purchasing 3 oz or 8 oz.

## Evidence and Guideline Alignment

### Professional guidance (category layer) — `app-aftercare`

APP Suggested Aftercare for Body Piercings strongly encourages **sterile saline labeled for wound wash**, with ingredients listing **0.9% sodium chloride** as the only ingredient (sometimes purified water). APP advises against DIY sea-salt mixes, harsh cleaners, over-cleaning, and using contact-lens saline, nasal spray, or eye drops. APP does **not** endorse any specific brand.

**Do not read this table as “APP-approved product.”** Overlap with APP framing on the **4.25 oz** label does not authorize extrapolating **0.9%** to every Steri-Wash size.

## Potential Advantages

Evaluated only against verified official claims—not clinical outcomes:

- **Water + sodium chloride** spray framing with **no drugs or preservatives** on official features.  
- **4.25 oz SKU:** label-verified **0.9% USP NaCl** and isotonic wording—useful for readers checking APP’s concentration guidance **for that size**.  
- **360° dispensing** and fine-mist / targeted-actuator framing (size-dependent copy).  
- Official claim that the product **remains sterile throughout the life of the canister**.  
- Size choice on official storefront: **3 oz**, **4.25 oz**, and **8 oz**.  
- **Made in the U.S.A.** per official features list.  
- **8 oz** wholesale listing: vegan friendly (SKU-specific marketing).  
- **3 oz** wholesale listing: “same medical grade sterile saline solution used in hospitals” (manufacturer marketing—not independent clinical proof).

These are **product-attribute advantages**, not proof of faster healing or fewer complications.

## Limitations and Uncertainties

- **Concentration is SKU-specific:** 0.9% USP NaCl is verified for the **4.25 oz** back label only—not confirmed for **3 oz** or **8 oz** on consulted front packaging.  
- Wholesale **“.09%”** page copy is **not** used here as concentration proof.  
- **No verified healing or infection-prevention outcomes.**  
- **Oral piercing suitability** not specified on pages consulted.  
- **No universal rinse schedule** in APP guidance; follow piercer and on-pack directions for the SKU purchased.  
- **8 oz** wholesale use language includes burns, wounds, piercings, and moistening dressings—treat as manufacturer use framing, not a substitute for piercer/medical advice.  
- **3 oz** “medical grade / hospitals” framing is manufacturer marketing.  
- Individual responses vary; saline cannot compensate for unsuitable jewelry or incorrect aftercare practice.

## FAQ

**Q: Is Steri-Wash 0.9% sodium chloride?**  
A: **Yes for the verified 4.25 oz back label** (Purified Water, 0.9% USP NaCl; isotonic label wording). **Not verified** here for **3 oz** or **8 oz**. Check the can you buy. Do not rely on wholesale “.09%” marketing copy as proof.

**Q: How often should I use it?**  
A: APP guidance does not give a fixed universal daily schedule for all piercings. Follow your piercer and the directions on the specific Steri-Wash SKU.

**Q: Is it the same as APP-preferred packaged sterile saline?**  
A: It overlaps on packaged sterile saline, water + sodium chloride, no drugs/preservatives, and spray use. **0.9%** is documented on the **4.25 oz** label; full-line concentration match is **unresolved**. APP does not endorse brands.

**Q: Can I use homemade salt water instead?**  
A: APP suggested aftercare no longer recommends DIY sea-salt mixes; they are commonly too salty and may over-dry the piercing. Prefer packaged sterile saline wound wash where available.

**Q: What if I have irritation or worry about infection?**  
A: Do not self-diagnose from an article. Contact a piercer or healthcare provider for concerning or worsening symptoms.
